The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King (2003)

In the depths of Middle-earth's history, the tragic tale of Sméagol, who becomes the twisted creature Gollum, unfolds with the discovery and murder over the One Ring. Centuries later, during the War of the Ring, Gandalf leads a coalition to Isengard, gathering allies and warning of Sauron's impending assault on Gondor. Meanwhile, Frodo, Sam, and Gollum journey towards Mount Doom, their friendship tested by the Ring's influence and Gollum's treachery. As Aragorn rallies forces for Gondor, he embraces his destiny as its rightful king, aided by the oath-bound spirits. In the ensuing battle, Théoden falls, but Éowyn and Merry slay the Witch-king, turning the tide. Aragorn's diversion allows Frodo to reach Mount Doom, where he succumbs to the Ring's power, only to be saved by Gollum's final act. With Sauron defeated, Aragorn is crowned king, Frodo finds peace in the Undying Lands, and Sam returns home to a new chapter of happiness.
